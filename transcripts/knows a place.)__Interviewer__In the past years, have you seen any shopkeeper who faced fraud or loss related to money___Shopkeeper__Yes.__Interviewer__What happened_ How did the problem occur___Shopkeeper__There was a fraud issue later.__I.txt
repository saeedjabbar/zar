NAIK AAMAL 

Interviewer:
Peace be upon you. How are you?

Shopkeeper:
All praise to Allah, I am fine.

Interviewer:
All praise to Allah. How are you?

Shopkeeper:
I am fine too.

Interviewer:
Actually, I am doing a survey related to a local business, so I will ask you some questions. You will answer them, and I will also record them. May I do that?

Shopkeeper:
Yes, you can do it.

Interviewer:
Okay. How long have you been running this shop?

Shopkeeper:
It has been about six to seven years.

Interviewer:
Mashallah. How many customers come in one day?

Shopkeeper:
Around one hundred customers come. Sometimes fifty come, but it is never very low. Usually, it is more than one hundred.

Interviewer:
Alright. During which part of the day is there more rush? Is it in the morning or in the evening? At what time do more customers come?

Shopkeeper:
There is more rush in the evening. There are customers during the day as well, but fewer. In the evening time, the rush is more.

Interviewer:
How do customers pay you? Do they mostly pay in cash?

Shopkeeper:
Yes, mostly they pay in cash.

Interviewer:
Do you take online transfers or online payments?

Shopkeeper:
Yes, we take online payments.

Interviewer:
Which mobile apps do you use for online payments?

Shopkeeper:
Mostly we use Easypaisa and JazzCash.

Interviewer:
And bank transfer?

Shopkeeper:
Yes, bank transfer as well.

Interviewer:
When did you start taking online payments? About how long has it been?

Shopkeeper:
(It has been some time.)

Interviewer:
Before taking online payments, what were you mostly afraid of?

Shopkeeper:
(Concern about problems/fraud.)

Interviewer:
Why did you start taking online payments?

Shopkeeper:
(Because customers prefer it.)

Interviewer:
Do you still face any problems while taking online payments?

Shopkeeper:
Yes, sometimes there are issues.

Interviewer:
Has there ever been a customer who asked you to transfer money for them after giving you cash? Have you ever done that?

Shopkeeper:
(Yes / it has happened.)

Interviewer:
Other than this, has any customer come who had money in dollars and asked you to exchange it?

Shopkeeper:
(It has happened.)

Interviewer:
Do you know any shop or service where a customer can exchange dollars if they do not have Pakistani cash?

Shopkeeper:
(Yes / knows a place.)

Interviewer:
In the past years, have you seen any shopkeeper who faced fraud or loss related to money?

Shopkeeper:
Yes.

Interviewer:
What happened? How did the problem occur?

Shopkeeper:
There was a fraud issue later.

Interviewer:
After that incident, did you avoid taking online payments because of fear of fraud, or do you still accept them?

Shopkeeper:
We still accept them.

Interviewer:
When you first started this business, what items did you keep in your shop at the beginning?

Shopkeeper:
At first, we kept basic items.

Interviewer:
For example, when you opened the shop, what items were there first, and later what new items did you add?

Shopkeeper:
Gradually, we added more products.

Interviewer:
Are you thinking of expanding your shop further?

Shopkeeper:
Yes.

Interviewer:
Do you make profit?

Shopkeeper:
Yes.

Interviewer:
In the past years, was there more profit, or is there more profit now? Is the profit increasing or decreasing?

Shopkeeper:
It is increasing gradually.

Interviewer:
Is your business going towards improvement or loss?

Shopkeeper:
It is improving.

Interviewer:
If you expand your business, what more items will you add?

Shopkeeper:
(More products.)

Interviewer:
Alright, thank you very much for your time. May Allah give more success to your business.
Okay, Allah Hafiz.
