


Interviewer: Hello, how are you?
Shopkeeper: Hello, I am fine. How are you?
Interviewer: Alhamdulillah, I am also fine.

Interviewer: I am doing a survey on a local business, so I will ask you some questions. I will also record your answers. Do I have your permission to ask you questions?
Shopkeeper: Yes, you can ask.

Interviewer: Okay. What items do you keep in your shop?
Shopkeeper: We have different types of items available. Grocery items and other daily-use products are available here.

Interviewer: Okay, that’s good. How long have you been running this shop?
Shopkeeper: I have been running this shop for about ten years.
Interviewer: Mashallah.

Interviewer: Approximately how many customers come to your shop in one day?
Shopkeeper: Many customers come here. Sometimes around 100, sometimes 200, and sometimes even more. But most customers come later in the day.

Interviewer: Okay, at what time do more customers come? When is your shop more crowded?
Shopkeeper: Most customers come after Fajr. Not many people come in the early morning.
Interviewer: Okay, that’s clear.

Interviewer: When customers buy something from you, how do they pay?
Shopkeeper: Some customers pay in cash, and some customers pay through online payment.

Interviewer: Which mobile payment services do you use?
Shopkeeper: We use EasyPaisa and JazzCash. We also use simple online bank transfer.

Interviewer: Okay. Does mobile payment make transfers easier?
Shopkeeper: Yes.

Interviewer: When you started taking mobile payments from customers who didn’t have cash, did it feel helpful? When did you start this?
Shopkeeper: When I started, I saw other shopkeepers using it. After that, I also started using it.

Interviewer: When you first started taking online payments, did you feel worried or stressed? Was there anything that made you afraid?
Shopkeeper: Yes, I was afraid of fraud.

Interviewer: Has any fraud ever happened to you with online payments?
Shopkeeper: No, no fraud has happened.

Interviewer: Since when have you been taking online payments?
Shopkeeper: I started after seeing other shopkeepers using it.

Interviewer: Do you face any problems with online payments now?
Shopkeeper: No, I have not faced any problems yet.

Interviewer: Has any customer ever asked you for help in transferring money online? Did you help them?
Shopkeeper: Yes, if someone has a problem, we help them and transfer the money.

Interviewer: Has any customer ever come to you with dollars and asked you to exchange them into local currency?
Shopkeeper: No, I have not done that.

Interviewer: So you don’t do currency exchange?
Shopkeeper: No, we don’t.

Interviewer: Do you know any shops nearby where currency exchange is done?
Shopkeeper: No, I don’t know.

Interviewer: If a customer comes to you and asks where they can exchange money, how would you help them?
Shopkeeper: I don’t really know about such shops here.

Interviewer: Have you ever heard about someone being cheated or facing fraud in money matters? Have you seen any such case?
Shopkeeper: No, I haven’t experienced or seen such a case.

Interviewer: Do you have any close family member living abroad who sends you money or runs a business there?
Shopkeeper: No, no one.

Interviewer: When you first started this shop, what items did you keep? How much investment did you start with?
Shopkeeper: In the beginning, I started with around two to five lakh rupees. After that, the business increased gradually, and the shop improved.

Interviewer: Mashallah, the business is running well.
Shopkeeper: Yes, Mashallah.

Interviewer: Thank you very much for your time. May Allah give you more success.
Shopkeeper: Allah Hafiz.
Interviewer: Allah Hafiz.
